1.1Background of the Study

Technology has become an essential part of the food and beverage industry,
especially with the growing use of online ordering systems. These systems make it
easier for customers to place orders and give businesses a more efficient way to
manage their operations. Many coffee shops today use digital platforms that allow
customers to order quickly and choose from different payment options. However,
Herbrews Kape still relies on traditional ordering methods. This often results in
slower service, limited reach to potential customers, and challenges in handling
transactions. Without a digital system, it is also difficult for the business to properly
track and analyze sales data. To solve these problems, this study proposes the
development of an online ordering system for Herbrews Kape. The system will
support both cash and online payments, while also providing automated sales
tracking. This aims to improve overall service efficiency and help the business make
better, data-driven background
1.2Statement of the Problem
    1.2.1 General Problem
         Hebrew's Cafe currently relies on manual processes and separate systems in

managing orders, inventory, customer records, and financial transactions. These
practices result in inefficiencies, delays in service, inaccurate data recording, and
difficulty in monitoring overall business operations. The absence of a centralized system
limits the cafť's ability to deliver fast, reliable, and organized service to its customers.

    1.2.2 Specific Problems
          Order processing in the cafť is handled manually or through disconnected
             systems, resulting in delays and inefficiencies.

          Inventory tracking is not updated in real time, leading to stock shortages or
             overstocking of items.

          Customer records are not properly organized, making it difficult to track
             preferences and improve customer service.

          Financial transactions and reporting are done manually, increasing the risk of
             errors and inaccuracies.

          There is no centralized system that integrates sales, inventory, customer
             management, and reporting functions.
1.3Objectives of the Study
    1.3.1 General Objective
     The main objective of this study is to develop a centralized online ordering and
    management system that streamlines cafť's operations, including sales, inventory,
    customer management, and financial reporting.

    1.3.2 Specific Objectives
          Design and develop an online ordering system for faster and more accurate
             order processing.
          Implement a real-time inventory management feature to track stock levels.
          Create a customer management module to store and manage customer data.

          Develop an automated financial reporting system for accurate tracking of
             sales and expenses.

          Integrate all operational modules into a single, user-friendly platform.
1.4Scope and Limitations
    1.4.1 Scope of the Study
          Online ordering system for customers
          Sales and transaction management
          Inventory tracking and monitoring
          Customer record management
          Financial reporting dashboard

    1.4.2 Limitations of the Study
          The system will only be implemented for a single cafť setup.
          It requires stable internet connectivity for full functionality.
          Payment integration may be limited (e.g., cash or selected digital payments
             only).
          The system will not include advanced analytics such as AI-based forecasting.
          Mobile application support may not be included (web-based only).

1.6 Definition of Terms

    1.6.1 Operational Definitions

          Online Ordering System ≠ A feature that allows customers to place orders
             through a web interface.

          Inventory Management ≠ The process of tracking and managing stock levels
             within the cafť.

          Customer Records ≠ Stored data containing customer information such as
             name, orders, and preferences.

          Financial Reporting ≠ Automated generation of reports related to sales and
             transactions.

          Centralized System ≠ A single platform where all cafť operations are
             managed.
1.6.2 Conceptual Definitions

     Management System ≠ A system designed to manage and organize
         operations efficiently.

     Automation ≠ The use of technology to perform tasks with minimal human
         intervention.

     Database ≠ A structured collection of data stored electronically.
     User Interface (UI) ≠ The part of the system that users interact with.
     System Integration ≠ The process of combining different subsystems into

         one unified system.
